This is following the article about SM Goh's visit to Saudi Arabia:

SM Goh says his visit to Saudi Arabia has strengthened ties

Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong says his 4-day visit to Saudi Arabia has helped to forge closer economic and political ties with the country. Singapore hopes to manage the funds of wealthy individuals and institutions from Saudi Arabia if they decide to park their funds outside Europe and the United States. During his visit, the Senior Minister met key Saudi leaders and also enjoyed a taste of Saudi culture and hospitality. Mr Goh says he has achieved his main aim of signalling Singapore's interest to nurture stronger ties with Saudi Arabia. He said: "I must say that I've achieved my purpose, the Saudis are very warm. They took my visit seriously. In a way, they treated me like a PM from the arrangement of meetings to the hospitality provided to me. In a way, they're signalling to me they want to have better ties with Singapore. So this is a beginning of a stronger relationship between Saudi Arabia and Singapore." He added that the Middle East is undergoing change and is an important region that Singapore investors should explore.


Mr Goh plans to bring a business delegation in April or May to Saudi Arabia and invite a Saudi business delegation to Singapore to match opportunities. He feels fund management is a key area: "We believe that they park their funds mostly in London and Zurich and even to some extent New York before September 11. And naturally they want good returns from their funds. And post-Sept 11, they maybe looking towards Asia and see where they can increase the returns to their funds. So, I'm trying to catch their eye if they're looking at Asia because Singapore can manage their assets or funds of the wealthy individuals in Saudi Arabia."


To enhance Singapore's attractiveness as a financial centre, Singapore is looking into offering Islamic banking services and products. This will enable the Republic to take advantage of the diversion of funds from conventional banking into Islamic banking in the region. Mr Goh who's also the Chairman of Singapore's Monetary Authority, said the MAS is studying Islamic banking and will organise a seminar to build its knowledge. "We don't set out to compete against Malaysia. We set out to work with Malaysia on what products that they have, what we can buy from them and what we can sell to the investors. The funds available are huge. So, if we can attract some of that funds to Singapore, we can enhance our own financial centre status," he added.


Mr Goh also said that his visit to Saudi Arabia has dispelled some of his own misconceptions about its peoples. Although the people of Saudi Arabia are culturally conservative, many of them do recognise the need to embrace change and this promises fresh opportunities for others. - CNA
