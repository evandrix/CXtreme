Saudi Arabia

Abdul Aziz bin Abdul Rahman ibn Faisal Al Saud aka Ibn Saud (1880 - 1953)

- Founder and first monarch of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia
- Born in Riyadh in 1880,he followed his family into exile in Kuwait in 1890 after his family's lands were conquered by the Rashidi
- Recaptured Riyadh with only twenty men by assassinating the Rashidi governor of the city
- After conquering and consolidating control over the Nejd and Hejaz areas in the Arabian Peninsula in 1932, Ibn Saud proclaimed himself King of Saudi Arabia
- Ibn Saud had 52 children by eight different women, 37 of which were male and thus eligible to succeed him as Kings. In fact, Ibn Saud is the father of all the Kings of Saudi Arabia that have succeeded him

Sami Al-Jaber (1972 to present)

- A child prodigy, Al-Jaber began his professional career at the tender age of 15 at Al-Hilal, Saudi Arabia's top club
- He earned his first international cap two years later, and was an integral part of the team that qualified for the 1994 World Cup in USA, scoring a hattrick against Iran to give Saudi Arabia its first World Cup berth
- Al-Jaber also became the first Asian player to score a goal in two consecutive World Cups when he scored against South Africa in a 2-2 draw
- His impressive performances caught the attention of European scouts, and Al-Jaber created another first by being the first Saudi professional to play in English football when he spent five months on loan with the English first division side Wolverhampton Wanderers
- He returned to Al-Hilal after five months in England without making much impact due to injuries
- He has since retired from international football after Saudi Arabia managed to qualify for the 2002 World Cup

Abdullah bin Abdulaziz al-Saud (1924 to present)

- The Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia, he has also been acting as the de-facto regent and ruler of Saudi Arabia after his half-brother King Fahd suffered was incapacitated by a major stroke
- Unlike the powerful "Sudairi Seven" brothers who have been close to one another all their lives, Crown Prince Abdullah does not have any full brothers, but he cultivated close relationships with half brothers and nephews who also lacked family allies because they either had no full brothers or were isolated for some other reason
- Crown Prince Abdullah has always had good relations with the various leaders of the United States, meeting Gerald Ford in 1976, George H.W. Bush in 1987 and Bill Clinton in 1998. His many visits to the US have led to reports that Crown Prince Abdullah is considered a great friend - to both the US and the Bush family
- Crown Prince Abdullah is also known as a philantrophist - after hearing about the twins from a doctor, he paid for the surgery of the Polish conjoined twins which took place at the King Abd al-Aziz Medical City in Riyadh on 3rd January 2005. 14-month-old Daria and Olga Kolacz were successfully separated after the surgery which took 15 hours.
Egypt

Gamal Abdel Nasser
- President Of Egypt from 1954 to 1970
- Was one of the most influential leaders of the Arab world and foremost proponent of Arab Nationalism in the 1950s and 1960s
- His nationalisation of the Suez Canal in 1956 brought him great prestige and popularity in both Egypt and the Arab nations
- His popularity is exemplified after Egypt's defeat by Israel in the Six-Day War of 1967, where people took to the streets to demonstrate against his attempt to resign from his position
- He also led Egypt through the War of Attrition in 1969 to 1970, but died of a heart attack a few weeks after the end of the war in September 1970
