Money
Saudi Arabia

S$1 is approximately equal to 2.25 Saudi Arabia Riyals, which has been pegged to the US dollar at a rate of 3.75 Riyals to one USD since 1986.
Riyal banknotes are issued in the following denominations: 1, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, and 500 Riyals.
Smaller values are issued as coins with 1 Riyal = 100 Halalas.

Egypt

S$1 is approximately 3.5 Egyptian pounds
Egyptian paper money ranges from 25 Piastres (a quarter of a pound) up to a 1,000 pound note, although the most commonly used currency range is from a single pound to 100 pounds.

UAE

S$1 is approximately equal to 2.20 Arab Emirates Dirhams (AED).
The Arab Emirates Dirham has been pegged to the US dollar since November 1980, at a rate of AED 3.630 for selling one dollar and at AED 3.6690 for buying one dollar.
One AED is equal to 100 fils, and coins come in denominations of 1 Dirham, 1, 5, 10, 25 and 50 fils.
AED notes are in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500 and 1000.
